# Enriched Genes Per Chromosome Arm: Detailed Mapping Summary

## Overview

This document provides a chromosome arm-by-arm breakdown of the 72 genes driving GO enrichment signals in subtelomeric Pseudohomologous Regions (PHRs). These genes span 18 chromosome arms across 9 Leiden communities, revealing distinct functional and spatial organization patterns.

## Summary Statistics

- **Total genes mapped**: 72
- **Chromosome arms with enriched genes**: 18
- **Leiden communities represented**: 9 (C1, C2, C3, C5, C6, C9, C11, C12, C14)

## Functional and Community Patterns

### Major Functional Hotspots

1. **chr4q**: Massive MIR8078 tandem array (31 copies) in community C1
2. **chr19p**: Mixed OR/snRNP cluster (6 genes) in community C3
3. **chr11p**: OR gene cluster (5 genes) in community C3
4. **chr3q**: OR gene cluster (5 genes) in community C3

### Community Distribution

- **C1 (37 genes)**: Dominated by MIR8078 arrays on chr4q and chr10q
- **C3 (21 genes)**: Diverse functional composition across 6 arms, particularly rich in OR genes
- **C11 (5 genes)**: Small cluster across 4 arms with mixed OR/snRNP functions
- **Other communities (9 genes)**: Scattered individual or paired genes

### Implications

This detailed mapping reveals that functional gene clusters in PHRs are not randomly distributed but follow specific community structures that reflect evolutionary sharing patterns. The concentration of MIR8078 in community C1 and OR genes in community C3 suggests that different Leiden communities may have evolved specialized roles in housing particular types of regulatory elements.
