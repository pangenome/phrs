## Block 1 - Main Claim And Caveat

Title:

> Gene enrichment: report-backed biology, conservative statistics

Slide text:

- Canonical HPRCv2 community-family Fisher screen: 116 tested rows, 0 BH-significant rows.
- The strongest rows are candidate presence patterns, not definitive enriched classes: C3 OR and C7 MTCO both have BH q = 0.07118.
- The biology is still clear at the report/table level: recurrent OR4F architecture, D4Z4/DUX4L at C1, DDX11L/WASH/FAM138/IQSEC3 at C5, PAR1 coding genes at C15, acrocentric MTCO pseudogenes at C7, and a pseudogene/ncRNA-rich subtelomeric backbone.

Speaker note:

> I am separating two things that the draft blurred. The family Fisher screen is conservative and gives no BH-significant rows, so I will not use GO-like language as proof. But the report-backed gene content is still useful biology: it identifies recurrent gene-family architecture across communities and gives us concrete rows to cite.

## Block 3 - GO/ORA Method Contrast

Title:

> GO context is useful, but not the proof layer

Slide text:

- Standard GO/ORA tables are historical context; they collapse copy number and should be labeled "standard/deduplicated".
- Coding-only ORA recovers olfactory and GTP-related terms from a 9-gene query, useful for context but too small and not copy-aware.
- Exploratory copy-weighted ORA gives striking fold enrichments, but the method was flagged as anti-conservative unless revalidated with calibrated/permutation methods.
- Therefore: lead with HPRCv2 report-backed community-arm support; show GO/ORA only as method contrast.

Speaker note:

> The right hierarchy is report-backed gene architecture first, GO context second. The old GO outputs explain why OR and GTP terms came up in brainstorming, but they should not be presented as the canonical statistical result.
