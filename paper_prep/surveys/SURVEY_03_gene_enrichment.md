## 1. Key findings with metrics

### 1.1 Universe and partition
- **374 unique genes** annotated across **39 of 41 arms** (chr7_q and chr12_q have zero gene annotations because their similarity regions are confined to 5–25 kb at the telomeric tip — these belong to community **C4**, which therefore has 0 genes).
- **576 shared gene instances** counted across arms within communities (one gene contributes one instance per arm in which it appears).
- **93 community-specific genes** (occur in only 1 community); **216 genes in ≥ 2 communities**; the remaining 65 are intermediate.
- **15 arm-level communities** (Leiden, resolution 1.17). Reference: `community_summary_table.tsv` (15 rows).

### 1.2 Biotype composition (the dominant qualitative signal)
- Subtelomeres are dominated by **pseudogenes (28.6 – 86.4 %)** and **ncRNA (8.5 – 50 %)**; protein-coding fraction is low everywhere except **C15 (PAR1) at 32.1 %** and **C2 (chr10_p / chr18_p) at 21.4 %**.
- Extreme: **C1 (chr4_q / chr10_q) = 86.4 % pseudogene** — driven by the D4Z4 array (DUX4L1–DUX4L44, 22 DUX4L pseudogenes specific to C1).
- C4 (chr7_q / chr12_q) = no annotations.

### 1.3 Statistical enrichment (Fisher per gene-family × community)
- **116 Fisher's exact tests, BH-corrected**. Reference: `community_enrichment_fisher.tsv` (116 data rows).
- **No significant results survive multiple testing** (the `significant` column is `FALSE` for every row).
- **Smallest unadjusted p-values (all q ≈ 0.071, not significant):**
  - C3 ↔ GTF2IP (GTF2I pseudogene): p = 9.2 × 10⁻⁴, OR = ∞ (6 in / 8 out)
  - C3 ↔ WBP1LP, C3 ↔ OR (olfactory receptor): p = 2.45 × 10⁻³ each
  - C7 ↔ MTCO (mitochondrial pseudogene): p = 2.24 × 10⁻³
  - C3 ↔ FAM138, C3 ↔ SEPTIN, C3 ↔ "Other pseudogene": p ≈ 0.017–0.023
- **Conclusion the section draws:** the enrichments described in the narrative (MTCO in C7, DUX4L in C1, OR4F broadly across C3/C5/C8/C9/C11/C12/C14) are **qualitative presence patterns**, not statistically significant after BH-116.

### 1.4 Mapping to prior duplicon literature
- **Ambrosini et al. (2007) Table 1 (subtelomere-specific blocks):** 11 entries. **10 of 11** map cleanly to a present-day Leiden community by anchor arm; only **block 5 (chr2_p)** has no PHR signal because chr2_p was excluded at the ≥ 95 % identity threshold. Three are confirmed gene-level (block 2 → OR4F4/OR4F17 in C8; block 7 D4Z4 → DUX4L cluster in C1; block 11 IL9R → IL9RP1 in C3; block 12 → IQSEC3 in C5).
- **Ambrosini et al. (2007) Table 2 (subterminal families A–F):** **5 of 6** map (Family A / chr2_p excluded as above). Family C's DDX11L-like signature is confirmed across 3–4 arms of C5.
- **OR4F olfactory family (Mefford & Trask 2002):** 10 OR4F genes detected across **7 communities**; **OR4F5 and OR4F8P each present in 14 arms**. This is the most chromosomally pervasive shared gene family.
- **Mitochondrial pseudogenes (MTCO):** novel observation — C7 (acrocentric p-arms) is enriched in MTCO1P34/MTCO3P26/33/34, enabled by T2T-quality assemblies of acrocentrics.

### 1.5 Hub genes (≥ 3 communities)
- Top hubs: **RPL23AP45 (10 communities, 21 arms)**, SEPTIN14P22 (9 / 22), DDX11L16 (9 / 20), FAM138D (9 / 17), LOC101929828 (9 / 21), LOC102723681 (9 / 23), RPL23AP60 / RPL23AP87 / RPL23AP88 (9 / 18–19).
- Pattern: **RPL23A pseudogenes and SEPTIN14 pseudogenes are the most widespread duplicon backbone**, followed by DDX11L, WASH, MIR6859 families spanning 7–9 communities.

### 1.6 Community-specific gene highlights
- **C7 (acrocentric p-arms)**: 48 specific (most) — MTCO mitochondrial pseudogenes + rDNA-associated.
- **C1 (chr4_q / chr10_q)**: 26 specific — D4Z4 DUX4L pseudogenes (22 of 26).
- **C15 (PAR1)**: 10 specific — includes **SHOX** (the most clinically meaningful protein-coding hit in the section).
- C11: 4 (lncRNAs); C3 f7501: 3 (FAM41AY1/2); C14 PAR2: 1; C6: 1.

### 1.7 QC
- **29 singletons + 7 doubletons** across 20 communities / 25 arms — interpreted as rare structural variants / borderline cluster assignments, not systematic errors.

## 4. Methods (incl. copy-number-aware ORA work)

### 4.1 Method as deployed in the section (arm-level)
- **Annotations:** Liftoff gene models, restricted to PHR coordinates, grouped per arm (`annotations/*.rds` → `community_gene_enrichment.tsv`).
- **Community structure:** 15 arm-level Leiden communities (resolution 1.17) — derived upstream by `detect_communities.R`.
- **Counting:** within each community, count distinct gene names and gene "instances" (gene × arm pairs).
- **Statistical test:** Fisher's exact test per (community × gene-family) cell of a 2 × 2 contingency table (in-community arms vs outside-community arms × family present vs absent). 116 cells tested. **BH-adjusted q-values; significance threshold 0.05.** No cell passes.
- **Biotype roll-up:** Liftoff biotype tags grouped to {protein_coding, pseudogene, ncRNA} for the per-community biotype table.
- **QC:** singleton/doubleton sequences whose arm is not the dominant arm of their community (29 + 7 across 20 communities).
- **Scripts:**
  - `/moosefs/guarracino/HPRCv2/scripts/community/analyze-community-enrichments.R` (analysis, produces all TSVs)
  - `/moosefs/guarracino/HPRCv2/scripts/community/plot-community-enrichments.R` (11 plots)
  - `/moosefs/guarracino/HPRCv2/scripts/community/community-utils.R` (shared utilities — `get_enrichment_dir`, palettes, sorting)
- **Cross-references:** Ambrosini et al. (2007) Tables 1 & 2 mapped manually to the 15 communities (the two mapping tables in the section).

### 4.2 Copy-number-aware ORA workstream (parallel exploration)
A substantial body of work in the repo root explores **copy-number-weighted hypergeometric / GO enrichment** on the PHR gene set. Highlights:

- **Motivation:** subtelomeric gene families are multi-copy (1–672 copies, mean 33). Standard ORA collapses each family to one gene, hiding genomic investment. Copy-weighted ORA reveals olfactory/transcription/GTPase signals that standard ORA underweights.
- **Headline result (`copy_number_enrichment_final_report.md`, `copy_number_aware_enrichment_results.csv`):**
  - Sensory perception of smell (GO:0007608): **598-fold**, p < 10⁻¹⁶ (OR4F17 / OR4F3 / OR4F5 / OR4F29; 58 copies).
  - Transcription regulation (GO:0006355): **928-fold** (DUX4 / FRG2 / FRG2B; 54 copies).
  - GTP binding / GTPase activity (GO:0005525 / 0003924): **309-fold** (GTPBP6 / IQSEC3; 18 copies).
  - Structural constituent of cytoskeleton: **825-fold** (ACTG1 / TUBB8; 16 copies).
- **Method docs:** `copy_number_enrichment_methods_report.md`, `copy_number_weighted_phyper_mathematical_formulation.md`, `copy_number_weighted_ora_master_synthesis.md`, `mathematical_framework_copy_weighted_ora.md`, `copy_number_weighted_ora_edge_case_analysis.md`, `copy_number_weighted_ora_best_practices_final.md`.
- **Implementation scripts:** `copy_weighted_enrichment.R`, `improved_copy_weighted_enrichment.R`, `robust_copy_weighted_enrichment.R`, `phr_copy_weighted_enrichment.R`, `copy_number_phyper_mapping.R`, `copy_number_enrichment.py`, `copy_weighted_robustness_tests.R`.
- **Literature / tool survey:** `existing_copy_number_aware_enrichment_tools_survey.md` (GAT, rGREAT, GREAT investigations: `gat_investigation_report.md`, `great_rgreat_investigation.md`).
- **Validation:** `corrected_statistical_validation.R`, `corrected_weighted_phyper_validation.RData`, `enrichment_validation_tests.R`, `comprehensive_edge_case_test_results.RData`.

The section as written **does not currently surface this copy-aware ORA**. Whether to fold it in is a synthesis question (see §5 + §6).
