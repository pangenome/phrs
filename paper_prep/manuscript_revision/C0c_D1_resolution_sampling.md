# C0c/D1 Resolution Scan and Sampling Stability

Date: 2026-06-17

Task scope: locate the existing Leiden resolution scans, sequence-level k/resolution scans, UPGMA comparisons, and any subsampling/stability outputs relevant to reviewer concerns C-0 and D-1. This note separates evidence for **resolution dependence** from evidence for **sampling stability** of the similarity matrix and community assignments.

## Bottom Line

The repository and moosefs outputs already support a precise statement about the **parameter dependence of the chosen partitions**:

- Arm-level Leiden was scanned from resolution 0.1 to 3.0 in 0.01 increments on the 41-arm distance matrix. The selected point is resolution 1.16, 15 communities, modularity 0.00714, mean silhouette 0.34734.
- Sequence-level Leiden was scanned over k-NN values and resolutions. If modularity is maximized without a community-count constraint, the scan prefers k=10/resolution=1.1 with 148 communities. The reported 50-community partition is therefore a constrained operating point: k=75/resolution=0.8, modularity 0.97053, with n_communities <= 50.
- UPGMA comparisons exist and are useful algorithmic robustness checks, but they are not sampling-stability tests. Arm-level UPGMA gives k=14 and agrees with 12 of 15 Leiden communities. Sequence-level UPGMA peaks at k=150, silhouette 0.63469.

The existing outputs do **not** yet prove sampling stability of the Leiden community assignments. The current evidence tests parameter choice and algorithm choice on a fixed similarity matrix. A true D-1 sampling-stability test would resample PHRs and/or samples/haplotypes, recompute the similarity matrix or k-NN graph, rerun Leiden with the same selection rule, and report ARI/NMI/co-clustering stability against the baseline partition.

## Resolution Dependence

### Arm-Level Leiden

Source: `/moosefs/guarracino/HPRCv2/PHR_III/similarity/hprcv2.1Mb.subtelo.arm-leiden.leiden_scan.tsv`.

Scan parameters:

- Input: 41 x 41 arm-level Jaccard distance matrix.
- Graph weighting: manuscript Methods state `w_ij = exp(-d_ij / median(d))`.
- Resolution grid: 0.1--3.0 in 0.01 increments.
- Selection rule: maximum mean silhouette.

Selected row from `C0_continuum/arm_leiden_best_resolution.tsv`:

| resolution | n_communities | modularity | silhouette |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 1.1600000000000001 | 15 | 0.007138870932518948 | 0.34733740233177857 |

The 15-community state is not a single-resolution artifact: `C0_continuum/arm_leiden_resolution_by_k.tsv` shows 15 communities from resolution 1.13 through 1.18, with the best silhouette at 1.16. Lower resolutions collapse communities: 1 community from 0.10 through 0.95, 2 communities at 0.96--0.97, and gradual fragmentation after that. Higher resolutions over-fragment: 39 communities by 1.95--2.15, 40 communities by 2.16--2.72, and 41 communities by 2.73--3.0.

Interpretation: the scan proves the selected arm partition is parameter-dependent in the normal sense for Leiden, and it identifies a local plateau around k=15. It does not by itself show that the 15 communities are invariant to resampling of PHRs, samples, haplotypes, or alignments.

## Sampling Stability

### What Already Exists

The closest existing sampling-style result is `scripts/cladistics/char_bootstrap_d_m9.R` and its report `paper_prep/synthesis/ANALYSIS_D_M9.md`.

That analysis:

- resamples PHRs with replacement;
- uses `/moosefs/guarracino/HPRCv2/PHR_III/all-vs-all.p95.id95.len.tsv`;
- rebuilds an arm-level distance surrogate from cached PHR-level cross-chromosome involvement;
- runs NJ and UPGMA per replicate;
- reports B=1,000 support for named groupings.

Important limitation for D-1: the D-M9 bootstrap is **not** a Leiden community stability analysis and does not recompute the canonical PGGB sequence-level Jaccard matrix. The report itself notes that the per-replicate distance is a defined surrogate because the accessible table stores chromosome-level involvement rather than exact per-arm-pair PGGB closure. It is still useful as context: it shows that several named groupings are robust to PHR-character resampling, while acrocentric and tight q-arm support are more sensitive. But it cannot be cited as proof that the 15 Leiden communities or the 50 sequence-level communities are sample-stable.

### Lightweight Check Decision

No valid lightweight sampling-stability check was run from cached small matrices. The cached 41 x 41 arm distance matrix can support resolution scans, UPGMA/NJ ordering, perturbation, and arm-deletion sensitivity, but it cannot answer D-1 sampling stability because it has already collapsed over PHRs, samples, and haplotypes. Subsampling rows/columns of that fixed matrix would test arm deletion, not sampling variability in the similarity estimates or Leiden assignments.

The sequence-level distance matrix is cached as a 925 MB RDS (`/moosefs/guarracino/HPRCv2/PHR_III/similarity/hprcv2.1Mb.subtelo.dist_matrix.rds`) and the full sequence assignment table is 15,668 rows. Re-running sequence-level subsampling/Leiden on that matrix is feasible on a compute node, but it is not appropriate for an opportunistic head-node run.

### Slurm-Ready Follow-Up

Wrapper/specification: `paper_prep/manuscript_revision/C0_continuum/run_leiden_sampling_stability.slurm.sh`.

The script is intentionally a Slurm-ready guardrail rather than a silent placeholder. It exits unless a concrete implementation is supplied at `paper_prep/manuscript_revision/C0_continuum/run_leiden_sampling_stability.R`.

Minimum required outputs for a complete D-1 sampling-stability run:

- `arm_leiden_phr_bootstrap_partition_stability.tsv`: per replicate, selected resolution, n_communities, modularity/silhouette, ARI/NMI to baseline k=15 partition.
- `arm_leiden_phr_bootstrap_pair_coclustering.tsv`: per arm pair, baseline same-community indicator and bootstrap co-clustering probability.
- `sequence_leiden_sample_subsampling_stability.tsv`: per sample/haplotype subsample, retained PHR count, k/resolution, n_communities, modularity, projected ARI/NMI to baseline k=75/resolution=0.8/k=50 partition.

Recommended design:

1. Arm-level PHR bootstrap: resample PHRs with replacement, rebuild the canonical arm-level Jaccard distance from per-PHR or per-PHR-pair contributions, rerun the arm Leiden resolution scan 0.1--3.0 by 0.01, and compare each replicate to `/moosefs/guarracino/HPRCv2/PHR_III/similarity/hprcv2.1Mb.subtelo.arm-leiden-k15.assignments.tsv`.
2. Sample/haplotype subsampling: draw subsets of HPRC samples or haplotypes at fractions such as 50%, 70%, 90%; rebuild the arm distance matrix and sequence k-NN graph on retained PHRs; rerun the same selection rules.
3. Sequence-level stability: because labels can split/merge under subsampling, compare partitions with ARI/NMI on retained PHRs and report co-assignment stability of biologically named mixed communities, not only the raw number of communities.
4. Controls: include the existing D-M9 named-grouping bootstrap as context, but keep it separate from the Leiden stability table.

## Honest Manuscript Statement for D-1

Recommended text:

> Leiden resolution scans identified a stable arm-level operating point at 15 communities (resolution 1.13--1.18; selected resolution 1.16 by maximum silhouette), and UPGMA on the same arm-level distance matrix recovered the same major biological groupings with 14 clusters. At sequence level, the reported 50-community partition is a deliberately constrained k-NN Leiden setting (k=75, resolution=0.8) rather than the unconstrained modularity maximum, which yields a finer partition. These analyses show that the reported communities are not tied to a single unexamined resolution, but they do not by themselves measure sampling stability of the underlying similarity matrix; a full PHR/sample-resampling Leiden stability analysis remains a follow-up rather than a result claimed here.

Shorter version for Methods or response letter:

> We scanned Leiden resolution at both arm and sequence levels and used UPGMA as an algorithmic comparison; these scans assess resolution and method dependence on the fixed similarity matrices. We do not claim formal sample-resampling stability for the Leiden assignments in the current manuscript.
