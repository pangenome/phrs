## Section 1: Topic scope

Topic 04 covers the pseudoautosomal regions PAR1 (Xp/Yp, ~2.6–2.7 Mb) and PAR2 (Xq/Yq, ~320–400 kb) — the two terminal X-Y homology intervals that recombine in male meiosis and underlie pseudoautosomal inheritance. In the current manuscript, these clades appear as communities C15 (PAR1) and C14 (PAR2) recovered by the NJ tree and Leiden partition, and are cited in three locations: (1) draft abstract L18 as archetypal examples of inter-chromosomal sequence sharing; (2) draft Main L22 establishing obligate crossover (cites Rouyer1986, sexchrompars_acquaviva2020, sexchrompars_bellott2024); and (3) draft Main L28 where the NJ tree clade recovery is stated ("PAR1 (Xp, Yp) forms one clade; PAR2 (Xq, Yq) forms a second", cites sexchrompars_acquaviva2020). In the 3D analysis, C14 (PAR2) appears as the most nuclear-periphery community (radial 0.840, Main L36). The topic also anchors the claim (CONSISTENCY_AUDIT row 33) that PAR1 arms are TAR1-free, supporting the mechanism distinction between PAR-type X-Y exchange and repeat-mediated PHR exchange on other arms. The primary evaluation standard is claim C5 of the abstract (cladistic recovery of known relationships), which the PAR literature directly validates. The existing review covers papers through Poriswanish2025 and Bellott2024, the most recent entry being the March 2025 UK Biobank extended-PAR study.
