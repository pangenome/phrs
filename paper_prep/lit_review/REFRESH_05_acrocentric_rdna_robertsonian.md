## Section 1: Topic scope

Topic 05 covers the biology of the five human acrocentric chromosomes (13, 14, 15, 21, 22) — specifically their structurally unique short arms, which carry the nucleolus organizer regions (NORs) with 45S rDNA tandem arrays, flanking satellite DNA, the proximal and distal junction (PJ/DJ) elements, and the segmental duplications that permit inter-arm recombination. In the Nature draft these arms are the subject of the most heavily supported claim: community C7 is the five acrocentric p-arms, and the allele-vs-paralog Wilcoxon reverses sign exclusively at C7 (paralog closer than allele, p = 2.0×10⁻⁷), confirming that the five arms are sequence-interchangeable. The draft invokes this topic in draft paragraph 3 (C7 as "third clade consistent with rDNA arrays and Robertsonian recombination", citing `acrocentric_Altemose2022` and `acrocentric_Guarracino2025ape`) and paragraph 4 (C7 cross-arm rate, allele/paralog inversion, `acrocentric_rdna_robertsonian_bandyopadhyay2001`). The pedigree paragraph (paragraph 7) anchors the empirical exchange count: 133 gene-conversion-like patches in C7 (~90%). The topic thus spans three draft locations (P3, P4, P7) and underlies both the structural genomics and the population-genetic inference in the paper.
