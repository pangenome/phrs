## Section 1: Topic scope

Topic 01 covers the cytogenetic and early molecular foundations of subtelomere research across three eras: the pre-molecular era (1916–1969), the molecular cytogenetics era (1970–1995), and the mapping era (1995–2005). In the Nature draft, this material appears primarily in **paragraph P1** ("The chromosome ends of the human genome were the first regions in which inter-chromosomal sequence exchange was identified…"), which cites Brown 1990, Wilkie 1991, Trask 1991/1998 as the founding references for cross-chromosomal exchange, and invokes Rouyer 1986 (PAR1), the acrocentric short-arm literature, and the full FISH/BAC-walking citation block (Mefford 2001, MeffordTrask 2002, Linardopoulou 2005, Riethman 2001/2004/2008, Flint 1997, Ambrosini 2007). In the end-to-end report, topic 01 content is anchored in section 12 ("Confirmed literature claims"), specifically the confirmations of the Flint/Mefford two-domain model (report §12, items 1–2) and the quantitative recovery of the Mefford 2002 f7501 African enrichment result (report §12, item 3). The topic underpins draft claims **C2** (reference-free approach required), **C3** (dataset size requirement), **C4** (extended interchromosomal homology at all subtelomeres), and **C5** (named communities correspond to cytogenetic-era duplicon families).

**[yang2025chr2fusion]** Yang Z et al., 2025. Incomplete lineage sorting of segmental duplications defines the human chromosome 2 fusion site early during African great ape speciation. *Cell Genomics* 6:101079. PMID:41338219 / DOI:10.1016/j.xgen.2025.101079

**Claim it supports:** Draft P1 / report §12 — the review states that Ijdo et al. 1991 demonstrated that chr2q13 "flanking sequences were shared among modern chromosome ends — an ancient PHR preserved in interstitial position," and that "interstitial telomere sequences co-localize with PHR domain boundaries in the two-domain model." Yang et al. 2025 provides the first T2T single-base-pair resolution of this fusion site, identifying three distinct SDs that originated >5 million years ago, showing their differential ILS distribution in African great apes, and documenting that one SD has homology to "hypomethylated SD spacer sequence present in the subterminal heterochromatin of Pan but is completely absent subtelomerically in both humans and orangutans." This directly extends and contextualizes Ijdo 1991's observation that ancient subtelomeric sequences are preserved in interstitial position, adds evolutionary depth (ILS during speciation), and confirms that the chr2q13 fusion site carries subtelomeric-origin sequence that has undergone lineage-specific silencing.

**Recommendation:** ADD alongside `cytofound_Ijdo1991` wherever the chr2 fusion locus is cited in the draft or report. The Yang 2025 paper does not replace Ijdo 1991 — it extends the same locus to T2T resolution — but given that the current paper uses T2T-quality assemblies throughout, Yang 2025 represents the authoritative modern view of the locus the review invokes as an example of "ancient PHR preserved in interstitial position."
