### The Transcriptional Control Hub: DUX4/FRG2 and Disease

**The finding**: DUX4, FRG2, and FRG2B transcription factors show the strongest enrichment signal in our analysis (928-fold, p < 10⁻¹⁶) with 54 copies distributed across 18 chromosome q-arms. This represents the largest genomic investment by copy number in PHR regions.

**Known biology**: DUX4 is a well-characterized embryonic transcription factor that becomes pathologically activated in Facioscapulohumeral Muscular Dystrophy (FSHD). Under normal conditions, DUX4 is expressed only during early embryonic development and then silenced in all somatic tissues except testis and thymus. In FSHD, chromatin relaxation at the D4Z4 macrosatellite leads to aberrant DUX4 expression in skeletal muscle, causing RNA metabolism disruption, cell signaling defects, and ultimately muscle degeneration.

**Novel contribution**: While FSHD literature focuses on chromosomes 4q35 and 10q26, our analysis reveals DUX4 presence across 18 different chromosome arms with 54 total copies—a distribution that dramatically exceeds current genomic annotations. This suggests that subtelomeric exchange has distributed D4Z4-like elements far more broadly than previously recognized, potentially creating a genome-wide network of DUX4-containing loci that could influence disease penetrance and severity.
