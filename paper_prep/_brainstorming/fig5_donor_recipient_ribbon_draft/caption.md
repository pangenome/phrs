# Fig. 5 Caption

**Pedigree-resolved donor-recipient ribbons for candidate PHR exchange.**
PAN027 inherited a paternal haplotype from PAN011; the child query haplotype was
compared with a joint two-haplotype PAN011 father target. Each panel shows one
500 kb subtelomeric PAN027 recipient window above the PAN011 donor window(s)
selected by the best inter-chromosomal father match. Colored blocks mark 2 kb
query-space windows whose inter-chromosomal father match beats the
same-chromosome or homologous father match, and ribbons connect the recipient
window to the corresponding donor interval. Adjacent 2 kb windows are merged for
display when donor chromosome, haplotype, and sequence coordinates are
consistent and nearby. Black bars mark PHR/PAR intervals; in the Xp/Yp panel,
PAR1 is drawn across the full visible 500 kb p-tip window because the canonical
PAR1 interval extends beyond the zoom. Gray triangles indicate that the
chromosome continues outside the plotted 500 kb window. Panel A shows the male
Xp/Yp transmission, including PAR1 as an internal positive control for obligate
male sex-chromosome recombination. Panels B and C show two putative
recombination-compatible autosomal PHR exchanges, chr5q/chr1p and chr9q/chr3q,
respectively. PHR/community labels were assigned only after calling; the three
highlighted pairs are supported by the population arm-community/Jaccard analysis
(Xp/Yp C15, chr5q/chr1p C11, chr9q/chr3q C3).
