**Primary Gene Location and Assembly Context:**

- **Gene structure**: 4 coding exons encoding a predicted 444-amino acid protein  
- **Evolutionary context**: TUBB8 is **primate-specific**, found only in primates
- **Functional specialization**: Oocyte-specific β-tubulin isoform essential for meiotic spindle assembly

### Beta-Tubulin Gene Family Organization

**Multi-Copy Gene Family Structure:**
Human DNA contains **approximately 14 copies per genome of alpha- and beta-tubulin genes**, though β-tubulin in humans is coded by a large gene family with **15 to 20 members**. This multi-copy architecture includes:

- **Functional genes**: 2 functional α-tubulin genes and 3 functional β-tubulin genes
- **Pseudogenes**: Multiple pseudogenes distributed across chromosomes 1, 6, 7, 8, 9, and 13
- **Retroposons**: Many pseudogenes are retroposons lacking intervening sequences
- **Dispersed organization**: Related tubulin sequences dispersed across multiple chromosomes

### TUBB8 Paralog: TUBB8B

**Recognition of TUBB8B as Distinct Paralog:**
Literature confirms the existence of **TUBB8B as an important paralog** of TUBB8, validating our PHR analysis findings. TUBB8B represents a related but distinct β-tubulin gene, supporting the concept of multiple functional copies within the TUBB8 gene family.

### Assembly Impact on Copy Detection

**GRCh38 Assembly Limitations:**
Current literature does not extensively document multiple TUBB8 copies, likely reflecting **assembly limitations** identified in general genome assembly studies:

1. **Subtelomeric challenges**: TUBB8's chr10p15.3 location places it in subtelomeric regions historically difficult to assemble
2. **Repetitive sequence issues**: High similarity between β-tubulin copies creates mapping ambiguity  
3. **Gap presence**: GRCh38 contained gaps that may have concealed additional copies
4. **Pseudogene confusion**: Difficulty distinguishing functional copies from pseudogenes

**T2T Assembly Revolution (2022):**
The **T2T-CHM13 assembly**, released in 2022, represents the first complete human reference genome:
- **Complete gap closure**: Eliminates all assembly gaps including subtelomeric regions
- **Improved repetitive sequence resolution**: Better discrimination of similar paralogous sequences
- **Enhanced copy detection capability**: Superior ability to identify genuine multi-copy genes
