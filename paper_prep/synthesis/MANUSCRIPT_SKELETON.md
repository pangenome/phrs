# MANUSCRIPT SKELETON — Population-scale architecture and 3D organisation of human subtelomeric exchange

**Format target.** Nature article (~3,000 words main text; 4 main display items; up to 10 Extended Data items; Methods + SI). Built from the 13 surveys in `paper_prep/surveys/`.

**Working title.**
> "Population-scale subtelomeric communities mirror three-dimensional nuclear organisation across human and mouse"

**One-sentence pitch.** Across 233 HPRCv2 individuals (465 haplotypes), 41 chromosome arms partition into 15 inter-chromosomal subtelomeric communities; the same partition is recovered as enriched 3D contact across six technologies (bulk Hi-C, Pore-C, CiFi, Dip-C, sperm scHi-C, mouse meiotic Hi-C), is generated by the inter-chromosomal exchanges captured directly in two pedigrees, and recovers cytogenetic-era patterns (Mefford & Trask 2002; Flint 1997; Linardopoulou 2005; Ambrosini 2007) at quantitative resolution.

## Main text outline

### Abstract (~200 words)
1 sentence: cytogenetic-era studies (FISH on flow-sorted chromosomes, single-genome cosmid surveys) sketched a "patchwork" of subtelomeric duplicons whose population-scale architecture has been invisible. We analyse 18,827 telomere-anchored 500 kb flanks across 465 HPRCv2 haplotypes; 83 % carry inter-chromosomal sequence sharing, partitioning 41 arms into 15 communities and 15,668 sequences into 50 communities. Within communities, alleles are closer than paralogs in 8 of 9 multi-arm communities; the lone reversal — acrocentric p-arms (C7) — quantifies population-scale interchromosomal homogenisation. The same partition is recovered as enriched 3D contact across six technologies and two species; non-sharing arms invert the signal, ruling out incidental proximity. Inter-chromosomal exchanges captured in a T2T 3-generation pedigree fall in Leiden communities at 92 %, identifying the events that build the population structure. Cross-arm exchange frequencies recover out-of-Africa topology; subtelomeric architecture is a population-genetic record. We interpret the system as a self-reinforcing similarity ↔ 3D-proximity ↔ ectopic-exchange feedback loop, with PARs as the limiting case of a continuum that spans all 24 chromosome ends.

### Main text sections

**1. Introduction (~400 words).**
- Open with cytogenetic-era continuity (FISH on flow-sorted chromosomes; f7501; Flint two-domain; Linardopoulou hot spots; Ambrosini duplicons) → frame as four-decade pattern at progressively higher resolution. (`SURVEY_FRAMING Part 3`)
- PARs as named special case → PHRs as general case (Rouyer 1986).
- Three classes of question newly addressable: population-scale phylogeography of exchange; sequence ↔ 3D linkage; unified PHR/PAR view.
- One paragraph on limits of single-reference / FISH data; HPRCv2 enables population-scale, sequence-resolved, haplotype-resolved analysis.

**2. Population-scale subtelomeric communities (~600 words; → Fig 1).**
- Pipeline at one paragraph: 465 assemblies → 18,827 flanks → 15,668 PHRs → arm- and sequence-level Leiden.
- 15 arm-level communities recapitulate known biology (D4Z4, PAR1/2, acrocentric-p, f7501 cluster, OR4F).
- Three architectural categories — homogeneous (8/41), polymorphic (34/41), fully interchangeable (7/41).
- 6 of 6 originally FISH-characterised arms (chr4p/4q/16p/18p/20p/22q) confirm the two-domain model; extends to 39/48 arms.
- f7501 reproduction: fixed sites confirmed, 3 new AFR-enriched arms, chr2_q SAS, chr6_p AMR.

**3. Inter-chromosomal exchange leaves a population signature (~500 words; → Fig 2).**
- Allele vs paralog: arm identity is preserved in 8 of 9 communities (Wilcoxon p < 1e-300); C7 is the lone reversal.
- C7 silhouette = −0.029, conversion score 1.000 toward all four other acrocentric p-arms — complete homogenisation at population scale.
- Two-domain model: gradient in 99.7 % of individual haplotypes; piecewise breakpoints 15–445 kb; internal (TTAGGG)n islands within 25 kb of breakpoint on 11/19 testable arms.
- Population structure: AFR vs non-AFR Fst 0.10–0.15, non-AFR/non-AFR ≈ 0; 10/19 Fisher-significant pairs; out-of-Africa topology recovered.

**4. Three-dimensional nuclear organisation mirrors sequence communities (~600 words; → Fig 3).**
- Multi-tech convergence (Hi-C / Pore-C / CiFi / Dip-C / sperm / mouse meiotic Hi-C); 14 effect sizes all on the within-community-closer side of unity.
- Strongest single statistic: HG002 Pore-C B/W = 0.056, p = 3.9 × 10⁻⁸⁵; CHM13 per-pair ρ = 0.674.
- S_all negative control: non-sharing arms reverse to *farther* in 0/16 GM12878 + 1/20 sperm cells.
- Confound exclusions strengthen, never weaken, the signal — rules out nucleolar / PAR / D4Z4 as sole drivers.
- Flanking paradox: unique-sequence flanks 100 kb centromere-ward give *stronger* signal than PHR centres (HG002 13× stronger), ruling out multi-mapping artifact.
- Cell-cycle modulation: mitotic 3× stronger global W/B but 1.4× weaker per-arm-pair ρ.
- Cross-species: mouse zygotene Mantel ρ = 0.718; B/W 0.029–0.122 across 4 meiotic stages.

**5. Pedigree-resolved exchanges build the community structure (~400 words; → Fig 4).**
- WashU 3-gen T2T: 538 HQ inter-chr patches; 92 % within Leiden community; 16 crossover-like events; 133 gene-conversion-like tracts (~90 % in C7); 229 acros_like NAHR signatures.
- CEPH1463 4-gen (cross-assembler-validated): 11 robust parent features all in known communities; chr10/chr18 (Linardopoulou) found independently in NA12877 paternal *and* NA12878 maternal — same canonical exchange in unrelated individuals.
- RPE-1 single-individual: pipeline rediscovers t(X;10) translocation from sequence alone (positive control).

**6. Discussion (~500 words).**
- Causal feedback loop: 3 of 4 links supported by present data; 1 (similarity → future proximity) requires temporal data.
- D4Z4-CTCF-lamin model for C1 (chr4q/chr10q): peripheral radial 0.732, 0–15 kb sharing peak at D4Z4, DUX4L median 22 vs 0–2 outliers (p = 5.3 × 10⁻⁶).
- Nucleolar association for C7; bouquet hypothesis for initiation (mouse-extrapolated; human meiotic Hi-C is the "single most informative missing experiment").
- Honest null: recombination rate vs cross-arm affinity collapses to ρ = 0.00, p = 0.98 once 7 short-read-confounded acrocentric/PAR arms are excluded.
- PARs are PHRs at the limit; the genome-wide "exchange community" is the general case.

**7. Methods (Online Methods, ~2,500 words).** Pipeline; Leiden parameters; Hi-C/Pore-C/CiFi/Dip-C/sperm framework; flanking control; pedigree untangle; mouse pipeline; statistical conventions (BH-FDR, Mantel permutation, bootstrap).
