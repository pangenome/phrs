# Nature-compressed Abstract Texture Probe

## Final candidate abstract

Human subtelomeres carry recurrent duplications, gene families and structural variation, yet their interchromosomal relationships have been difficult to characterize because chromosome ends were incompletely assembled and standard alignments partition homologous sequence by chromosome. Here we survey subtelomeric sequence sharing across 465 near-complete assemblies (232 HPRC v2 individuals and CHM13) with an implicit pangenome graph that samples approximately 12% of haplotype-pair comparisons, then queries transitive relationships without chromosomal or positional priors. We find extended interchromosomal homology on 41 of 48 chromosome arms, spanning tens to hundreds of kilobases and 3.51 Mb outside acrocentric short arms and pseudoautosomal regions. These pseudo-homologous regions define 15 sequence-similarity communities that recover Xp/Yp, Xq/Yq, acrocentric, 10p--18p and 4q--10q DUX4 systems, and resolve a linked q-arm group involving 22q, 21q, 19q, 1q, 13q and 17q. Human Pore-C and Hi-C show that sequence-similar subtelomeres contact more often in three dimensions; mouse meiotic Hi-C shows that this correspondence peaks at zygotene, when telomeres cluster in the bouquet. A telomere-to-telomere human pedigree provides evidence consistent with recent exchange within these communities. Together, these data identify pseudo-homologous subtelomeres as near-ubiquitous substrates for concerted evolution and support a model in which nuclear architecture and recombination reinforce their persistence.

## Rationale for language choices

- Opens with the biological obstacle before the technical advance, matching the Nature-style problem-to-method cadence in the current abstract and voice plan.
- Uses active, exact verbs: "survey", "find", "define", "recover", "resolve", "show", "identify" and "support" each name the evidentiary action without promotional framing.
- Keeps the stable quantitative anchors explicit: 465 near-complete assemblies, 232 HPRC v2 individuals plus CHM13, approximately 12% sampled comparisons, 41 of 48 arms, 3.51 Mb and 15 communities.
- Compresses known positives and new findings into one sentence so validation systems and novel community structure appear as a paired result rather than separate claims.
- Softens the final mechanism to "support a model" and "reinforce their persistence", preserving the talk's chicken-or-egg caution while retaining the title-level theme of concerted evolution and unorthodox recombination.

## Words and phrases intentionally avoided

- "Map" and "chart": too vague for the analytical work; "survey", "characterize" and "resolve" state the action more precisely.
- "Directly observe interchromosomal exchange": stronger than the pedigree evidence supports and inconsistent with the stated caution that the signal is compatible but not definitive.
- "Prove", "demonstrate the mechanism" and "drive": these would overstate causal direction between 3D proximity, sequence homology and recombination.
- "Unbiased view": the method reduces chromosomal and positional priors, but the 12% sampled comparison design and assembly inputs still warrant measured wording.
- "Maintained by nuclear architecture": "support a model in which nuclear architecture and recombination reinforce" better preserves unresolved directionality.

## Claim-strength audit

- Pedigree evidence: "provides evidence consistent with recent exchange" is intentionally below "directly observes" or "proves". It allows the WashU telomere-to-telomere pedigree to contribute to the recombination model while preserving the baseline caution about assembly artefacts and non-definitive inference.
- 3D evidence: "show that sequence-similar subtelomeres contact more often in three dimensions" is a measured-contact claim for human Pore-C and Hi-C. The mouse sentence is limited to the zygotene peak and bouquet timing, not a claim that mouse meiosis proves human exchange.
- Mechanism: "support a model" and "reinforce their persistence" frame nuclear architecture and recombination as a plausible maintenance model, not a resolved causal pathway. This matches the talk's unresolved chicken-or-egg question between proximity and homology.
