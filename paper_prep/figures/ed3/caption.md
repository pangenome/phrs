# Extended Data Figure 3 — Annotation: TAR1, internal (TTAGGG)n islands, telomere length

**a, TAR1 prevalence per arm.** Bars show the percentage of haplotypes carrying ≥1 TAR1 entry on each of 41 chromosome arms (n=14,816 of 15,668 sequences with TAR1; 94.6%). PAR1 arms (chrXp 0.3%, chrYp 1.1%) are TAR1-free; acrocentric p-arms (chr13p–chr22p) sit at 73–79%; autosomal arms saturate at >99%. Source: `community_tar1_by_arm.tsv`.

**b, (TTAGGG)n island length and motif composition.** Length-bin histogram of 18,352 internal (TTAGGG)n islands across 8,321 sequences (53.1% of PHR sequences); mode is 50–74 bp (46.0%), only 7.9% of islands fall in the 150–200 bp range Ambrosini et al. 2007 reported as their mode. Inset hexamer composition (296,406 hexamers): 52.3% canonical TTAGGG, 47.7% degenerate variants (TGAGGG, TTGGGG, TCAGGG). Sources: `length_distribution.tsv`, `motif_composition.tsv`.

**c, Terminal telomere length by community.** Per-community medians (red) ± mean SD (whiskers), ordered by median. Range 7,638 bp (C10/chr17p) to 9,418 bp (C13/chr4p). Kruskal-Wallis H = 100.89, p = 3.2e-15 across 13,668 sequences in 15 communities. Source: `telomere_length_by_community.tsv`.

**d, Per-arm TAR1 distance-from-telomere (median, log10 kb).** Most arms place TAR1 within 1 kb of telomere; acrocentric p-arms (orange, 179–196 kb) and chr9q (223 kb) carry the deepest TAR1, reflecting their unusually long PHRs. PAR1 (red) excluded by ≤2 entries. Source: `tar1_positional_per_arm.tsv`.
